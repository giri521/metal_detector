Explore and learn about the properties of common metals used in daily life before detecting them with AI.

Gold

Color: Bright yellow
Uses: Jewelry, electronics, currency
Features: Non-reactive, high value

Gold is a precious metal used for wealth storage and luxury items. It is an excellent conductor and does not tarnish, making it ideal for high-end electronics and investments.

Silver

Color: White metallic
Uses: Jewelry, mirrors, electronics, medical
Features: Highest electrical conductivity

Silver is known for its brilliant shine and conductivity. It is widely used in solar panels, circuit boards, and antimicrobial medical applications.

Copper

Color: Reddish brown
Uses: Wiring, motors, plumbing
Features: Flexible, conducts heat and electricity

Copper has been used since ancient times for tools and decoration. Today it's essential in renewable energy, construction, and industry due to its conductivity and affordability.

Iron

Color: Grey
Uses: Construction, tools, vehicles
Features: Strong, magnetic, prone to rust

Iron is a fundamental building material. It's widely used for its strength, especially in construction and machinery, but it needs protection from rust.

Steel

Color: Silvery grey
Uses: Buildings, bridges, appliances
Features: Durable, rust-resistant, alloy of iron

Steel is made by refining iron and combining it with carbon. It is stronger and more corrosion-resistant, used in everything from skyscrapers to kitchenware.
